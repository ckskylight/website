Mobile and wearable technology

Android, Tizen Wearable, Android Wear, projects working with multiple wearable devices

Wearable technology is the inevitable future, and I've been increasingly involved in this space, testing out and developing on new devices and platforms.

2014: Kopin Internship


Worked with Kopin's Smart Eyewear and designed/developed a new version of the device's main menu interface that is more intuitive to navigate makes it easier to see text on the display in bright sunshine.


Developed on a Galaxy Gear Neo (Tizen Wearable OS) and LG G Watch (Android Wear) and integrated them with Kopin's eyewear platform, enabling navigation through menus by swiping the watch face. I also studied and compared the different architectures and presented on their various pros and cons.


Prototyped a smart bike helmet and bike application that connects to a Wahoo Blue SC Bike Sensor and a Wahoo Blue HR Heartrate Monitor using their Android API.


Created a turn-by-turn directions eyewear application that uses the Google Maps API and GPS to nofity users where and when to turn.


Documented all my work in a notebook and created documents that outline Kopin's approach to their eyewear SDK and benchmarking that against the architecture of other wearables such as the Galaxy Gear and the LG G. Presented a final demo of all my work to top company executives.


2014: Kubra (startup)


Co-founded Kubra with two others. Designed and created a prototype for a gesture control ring used for controlling wearables without the need for a touch or voice interface.


Winner of the audience choice award at the 2014 MIT 100K Accelerate competition and interviewed for Y Combinator.
